Understanding Star Types

Stars are classified based on their temperature, luminosity, and size. Below is an overview of different types of stars:

Star Type Overview

Star Type Temperature (K) Size LifeSpan Example Star
Brown Dwarf < 2000 Sub-stellar They never "die" since they aren't technically stars, but instead cool/fade over an extremely long period of time (billions of years) WISE 0855âˆ’0714
Red Dwarf 2500 - 4000 Small Billions to trillions of years depending on solar mass, making them the longest lived star type Proxima Centauri
Main Sequence 4000 - 10,000 Medium Average of ~10 billion years (such as our sun), but this also depends on solar mass Sun
White Dwarf 10,000 - 100,000 Small These are remnants of low/medium mass stars and will gradually cool for billions to quadrillions of years before becoming a black dwarf (note a black dwarf has never been observed, showing how long "lived" these are) Sirius B
Supergiant 3,500 - 40,000 Huge Approximately 5 to 20 million years before ending their lives in supernova explosions Betelgeuse
Hypergiant 3,000 - 50,000 Enormous Approximately 1 to 2 million years before ending their lives in supernova explosions VY Canis Majoris
